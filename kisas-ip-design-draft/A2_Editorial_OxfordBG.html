02 — IDENTITY MAP

Who am I?

What shapes my story? What do I want others to understand about me? Identity is a collection of people, places, memories, values, and dreams — constantly shaping who you are.

Today's Goal — Map the people, places, values, interests, and experiences that shape you. Use key words, short phrases, symbols, or simple drawings. You do not need to fill every box.

DAY 02 03
04 — PARTNER SHARE

Practice saying it out loud.

Use your identity map to prepare a short introduction. Before you share, review the checklist. After you share, fill in what you learned.

Partner Share Checklist

I shared 3 parts of my identity map with my partner.
I explained why one part of my identity matters to me personally.
I asked a question — one thoughtful, open-ended question about their map.
I listened respectfully and took at least one note about what my partner shared.
One thing I learned about my partner
One question I still have

30–45 Second Introduction Planner

Aim for honesty over perfection. The goal is not to sound polished — it is to sound real.

My name is
One important part of who I am is
This matters because
One thing I hope to grow during camp is

Authentic Voice Reminder — Sound real, not perfect. Use your own examples. Choose words you would actually say. Speak clearly, slowly, and with confidence.

2 · Why Names Matter

  • Using someone's name says: "I see you. You matter."
  • Names build connection and community.
  • Correct pronunciation shows care and inclusion.
  • It's okay to ask again and practice.

DAY 02 05
05 — CHORUS LAB

The part people remember.

CHORUS = the part people remember. Make 2 versions. 8–12 words. Clarity beats rhyme.

Quick CHORUS Builder.

Pick one frame. Fill the blank. Read it aloud. Clap the rhythm. Choose the one that feels true.

Frame 1 — Say my name — _________ (because _________).
Frame 2 — When you say my name, I feel _________.
Frame 3 — My name is my story — _________.
Frame 4 — You see me when you _________ (say it right).
